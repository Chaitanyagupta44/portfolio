Evidence-Based Leadership Tool for One-to-One Check-ins

Improving quality of Team Leader conversations on wellbeing, performance, and development

The Challenge

Team Leaders conducted monthly one-to-one check-ins using a 15+ page document. Survey results showed inconsistent use and cognitive burden. The organization needed evidence-based tools that encourage high-quality leader behaviors while being practical to use.

My Approach

I conducted a literature review on effective leadership behaviors and coaching frameworks (GROW model, Self-Determination Theory). I designed concise, behavior-focused guides that replace the lengthy document with practical prompts grounded in evidence.

Impact

Delivered within 4 weeks. Commissioner provided outstanding feedback, noting I went "above and beyond" and finding "nothing that could've been done better." Tool has potential for wider organizational rollout.

Methods

Literature review, GROW framework, Self-Determination Theory, organizational data analysis

Context

Duration: 4 weeks
Area: Employee Relations
