What I Do

Work Psychologist | UK Government Department for Work and Pensions

I currently work as a Work Psychologist within the Work and Health Services directorate, providing specialist employment-related psychological advice, assessment, and support to job-seeking customers and frontline colleagues including Work Coaches, Disability Employment Advisers, and Employer Advisers.

My day-to-day work involves applying occupational psychology across diverse contexts-from designing one-to-one assessments for vulnerable job-seekers to facilitating group interventions that build work-search skills and motivation. I support both customers progressing towards employment and employed individuals sustaining their roles.

Individual Assessment & Support

Designing and delivering one-to-one interventions to assess employment-related support needs for socially excluded, vulnerable, or disadvantaged job-seekers. Identifying suitable job goals, work solutions, and technological support to help customers find, sustain, and progress in work.

Group Interventions

Facilitating work-focused group interventions to help job-seeking customers build work-search skills, confidence, and motivation. Creating psychologically-informed approaches that support behaviour change and employment readiness.

Colleague Development

Designing and delivering training interventions to help colleagues increase their knowledge and develop job-relevant skills-such as interviewing and coaching techniques-that improve customer outcomes. Providing case conference facilitation and caseload management support.

Intervention Evaluation

Appraising the quality and appropriateness of interventions that DWP uses or is considering for procurement. Applying evidence-based frameworks to evaluate effectiveness and inform organizational decision-making.

I'm currently undertaking the Stage 2 Qualification in Occupational Psychology through the British Psychology Society, with supervision and support from DWP. This role allows me to apply psychological science to real-world challenges affecting some of the most vulnerable individuals in society, while developing my practice across all core areas of occupational psychology.

Performance & Assessment

Oral Briefing Assessment for UK Government Policy Recruitment

Designed a fair and accessible verbal communication assessment tool for recruiting policy roles in the UK civil service. Focused on assessing candidate potential over experience while reducing adverse impact on minority candidates through evidence-based, accessibility-oriented design.

Methods: Work sample validity, cognitive accessibility frameworks, fairness-focused selection methodology, neurodiversity considerations, structured scoring matrices
Deliverables: Assessment tool, scoring matrices, assessor guides for junior and senior roles
Duration: 6 months
Learning & Development

Work-Related Information Training for Front-line Advisors

Designed, delivered, and evaluated training for front-line advisors who coach vulnerable job-seekers. Upskilled advisors on exploring functional ability using open-ended questioning techniques, applying coaching psychology principles to improve support for customers with health conditions.

Methods: Kolb's Experiential Learning Model, Egan's Skilled Helper Model, Kirkpatrick's evaluation framework (Levels 1 & 2), training needs analysis, process consultation
Deliverables: 90-minute training sessions, evaluation surveys, facilitator guides
Duration: 6 months
Employee Relations

Evidence-Based Leadership Tool for One-to-One Check-ins

Designed evidence-informed leadership guides to improve the quality and consistency of Team Leader-led one-to-one check-ins. Created behaviour-focused tools that encourage high-quality leader behaviours around wellbeing, performance, and development discussions, replacing a cognitively burdensome 15-page document.

Methods: GROW coaching framework, Self-Determination Theory, meta-analyses on effective leadership and feedback processes, organizational data analysis, process consultation
Deliverables: Short behaviour-focused guide for Team Leaders, detailed usage guidance, ethical guidelines for one-to-ones
Duration: 4 weeks
Wellbeing at Work

Team-Based Resilience Intervention for Public Sector Staff

Designed, implemented, and evaluated a sustainable resilience intervention for public sector employees experiencing high stress, anxiety, and emotional labour. Created a Team Leader-led intervention promoting evidence-based resilience behaviours through habit formation, allowing for scalability without sustained specialist involvement.

Methods: Job Demands-Resources Model, Psychological Capital framework, PERMA model, staff survey analysis, joint diagnosis, RE-AIM evaluation framework
Deliverables: Team Leader-led intervention materials, implementation guides, evaluation framework
Context: Local office (100+ staff) in UK public sector organization
Organizational Development

National Evidence Review: Neurodiversity-Inclusive Work Design

Conducted systematic review of academic literature on neurodiversity at work for a national-level evidence-based report. Translated complex evidence into actionable, plain-language recommendations focused on system-level work redesign and inclusive organizational practices, moving beyond "fix-the-person" approaches.

Methods: EPPI-style systematic review, AORTA appraisal framework, Job Demands-Resources theory, Procedural Justice Theory, Universal Design principles, thematic synthesis
Deliverables: Evidence synthesis, work design recommendations for organizations and policymakers, good practice guidance across work functions
Duration: 4 weeks

About Me

I'm a Occupational Psychologist with expertise in designing evidence-based solutions across the five core areas of occupational psychology. My work focuses on creating fair, accessible, and psychologically-informed interventions that support both organizational goals and individual wellbeing.

Currently working as a Work Psychologist within the UK government, I specialize in assessment design, learning and development interventions, and applying psychological principles to solve complex organizational challenges. My practice emphasizes ethical decision-making, rigorous evidence appraisal, and reducing adverse impact on vulnerable and minority groups.

I hold an MSc in Occupational Psychology and am undertaking the Stage 2 Qualification in Occupational Psychology through the British Psychology Society. My approach is grounded in consultation models, systematic evidence review, and a commitment to translating psychological science into practical, real-world impact.
