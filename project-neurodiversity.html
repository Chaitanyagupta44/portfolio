National Evidence Review: Neurodiversity-Inclusive Work Design

Systematic review translating academic evidence into organizational recommendations

The Challenge

Commissioned to produce national-level, evidence-based report on neurodiversity at work. A large mixed-quality evidence pool needed systematic review and conversion into actionable, plain-language recommendations for organizations and policymakers.

My Approach

Conducted EPPI-style systematic review of academic literature using AORTA appraisal framework. Triangulated themes across evidence types. Structured recommendations through Job Demands-Resources theory, Procedural Justice Theory, and Universal Design principles-moving beyond "fix-the-person" approaches to system-level work redesign.

Impact

Created comprehensive evidence synthesis and work design recommendations. Client highlighted "thorough preparation", "clear alignment with brief", and strong application of frameworks to identify root causes. High potential for adoption given prominent client in neurodiversity field and increasing organizational demand for neurodiverse workforce support.

Methods

EPPI systematic review, AORTA framework, Job Demands-Resources theory, Universal Design principles

Context

Duration: 4 weeks
Scope: National-level
Area: Organizational Development
