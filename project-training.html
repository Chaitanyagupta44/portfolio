Work-Related Information Training

Upskilling advisors to support vulnerable job-seekers through evidence-based coaching

The Challenge

Front-line advisors coach vulnerable job-seekers with health conditions affecting their capacity for work. While expected to provide psychological coaching, most advisors had limited training. They needed upskilling on exploring functional ability using open-ended questioning techniques.

My Approach

Training Design

I designed a 90-minute training using:

  • Kolb's Experiential Learning Model (content → reflection → application)
  • Egan's Skilled Helper Model for questioning techniques
  • Kirkpatrick evaluation framework (Levels 1 & 2)

Training Objectives

  • Increase advisor knowledge and confidence in using open-ended questioning
  • Explore functional ability in customer conversations
  • Apply coaching psychology principles

Impact

  • Improved advisor confidence and skills in functional ability assessment
  • Scalable training materials for organizational rollout
  • Pilot for wider implementation across UK regions
